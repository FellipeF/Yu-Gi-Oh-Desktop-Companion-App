Why some cards appear as “anime-only” and deck differences.

1) Why some cards are “anime-only”

Some decks (especially early anime arcs) include cards that never existed in the TCG/OCG, have different names, or are not present in the API dataset provided by YGOProDeck. When the app can’t match a deck entry to a known card ID, it stores the entry as card_name only. These are displayed as “anime-only”.

Cards are always shown with images when a real card_id exists. If the card is “anime-only”, a placeholder is shown.

Common reasons

  • The card is fictional (anime/manga-only) and was never printed.
  • The card exists, but the deck list name differs from the database name (spelling / punctuation / localized naming).
  • The card exists, but is missing in the selected dataset/language.

2) Anime rules vs TCG/OCG rules

The anime often uses “story rules” that do not match the real card game rules (TCG/OCG). That’s why a character’s anime deck list can look “wrong” compared to an official playable deck.

Example: Fusion monsters used like normal monsters

In early arcs, you may see fusion monsters appear without a fusion spell, or treated like normal summons. This is an anime storytelling choice and not valid under standard TCG/OCG rules.

Other common differences

  • Cards with effects that never existed in print, or effects that were later changed (errata).
  • Early “rule experiments” (field power bonuses, unusual win conditions, etc.).
  • Character-specific cards that were renamed or redesigned when printed.
